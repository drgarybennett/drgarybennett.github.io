About Gary Bennett

Gary G. Bennett, Ph.D., serves as Dean of Trinity College of Arts & Sciences at Duke University, the institution’s largest and most comprehensive academic unit. Trinity comprises 35 departments and programs across the arts, humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences; educates nearly 6,000 undergraduates; and supports Duke’s largest doctoral student population. With nearly 700 regular-rank faculty, almost 600 staff, approximately 1,600 graduate students, and an operating budget approaching half a billion dollars annually, Trinity Colege delivers a robust and contemporary liberal arts education within a world-class complex research organization.

University-Wide Leadership

Prior to becoming dean, Dr. Bennett served as vice provost for undergraduate education, where he provided strategic vision and leadership for many facets of Duke’s undergraduate experience. He oversaw a 15-unit portfolio spanning advising, academic support, financial aid, study abroad, nationally competitive scholarships, and co-curricular programs.

Under Bennett’s leadership, Duke raised tens of millions of dollars to expand access and innovation in undergraduate education. Dr. Bennett led the development of DukeLIFE, supporting first-generation and low-income students, and QuadEx, Duke’s inclusive living-and-learning model integrating residential, social, and intellectual life.

Research

Dr. Bennett is a nationally recognized scholar in digital health and behavioral medicine, with nearly 200 scientific publications and continuous funding from the National Institutes of Health. Nearly two decades ago, he established one of the first digital health research programs, now internationally recognized for translating rigorous science into scalable, technology-enabled interventions deployed in real-world clinical settings.

Dr. Bennett’s field leadership is further exemplified by his tenure as president of the Society of Behavioral Medicine and his election to prestigious bodies such as the Academy of Behavioral Medicine Research and the Behavioral Medicine Research Council.

From Ideas to Impact

Dr. Bennett’s work extends beyond scholarship to building and leading mission-aligned enterprises that translate ideas into durable impact. He has co-founded three digital health ventures grounded in rigorous science and designed to operate at scale. Crimson Health Solutions, which developed digital disease management interventions, was acquired by Health Dialog in 2007. Scale Down, a digital obesity treatment company, was acquired by Anthem in 2017. He is also a co-founder of Coeus Health, a leading provider of health APIs supporting large-scale digital health infrastructure. He has served as a science advisor and board member to several consumer electronic and digital health companies, including Apple, Google, Weight Watchers, Wondr Health, and Lore.

Across these efforts, Dr. Bennett has served as a senior leader, helping to scale organizations with responsibility for strategy, budgets, and execution. He has led multidisciplinary teams, managed growth, and helped guide organizations through acquisition while maintaining mission alignment and financial sustainability. He brings this operating experience into academic leadership, applying management discipline and long-term stewardship in service of higher education’s core mission.

Education and Academic Roles

Dr. Bennett began his academic career on the faculties of the Harvard School of Public Health and the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute, where he conducted research on the digital treatment of obesity in high risk populations. He joined Duke University in 2009.

He earned a bachelor’s degree from Morehouse College, an AM and PhD in clinical health psychology from Duke University, completed a clinical internship in medical psychology at Duke University Medical Center, and was the Alonzo Yerby Postdoctoral Fellow in Social Epidemiology at the Harvard School of Public Health.
